Attendance

Absences and Tardiness

When a child is late it has an impact on his/her education. All classrooms have important established routines. Missing all or part of that routine has a negative impact on the child’s day. In addition, arriving late disrupts other students and the established routine. Tardy students are distracting and cause others to lose their focus. Students and teachers are robbed of valuable time. The following is Trombly’s tardiness/absence policy:

 

  • 3 tardies- call home from the office
  • 5 tardies – student loses recess privileges
  • 8 tardies – student loses lunch and recess privileges
  • 10 tardies – student stays 30 minutes after school and loses two recess periods
  • 15 tardies – truancy proceedings will be started.

 

Just as with tardies, students who are frequently absent lose out. If a child accumulates three or more absences within a month, a discussion will be initiated about the nature of the absences and how the school might be of assistance in addressing the child’s attendence issues. Trombly’s absence line is 432-5001.

* Please note: if I do not have your child in my sight by 8:27 for the a.m., or 12:27 for the p.m. he or she will be marked tardy or absent!

Classroom Rules and Behavior

Students are expected to be: respectful, responsible, and ready to learn. Respectful students use polite words such as please, thank you, may I and excuse me, they give compliments not criticisms, they listen while another person is speaking, they do not interrupt, they take turns and share. They willingly include others in group activities within the classroom and on the playground. Responsible students stick to a task, follow directions, work within time frames and can be counted on to take care of business without creating disruptions or problems. They use the Debug system or other problem solving strategies to resolve conflicts peacefully. Responsible students try their personal best to follow school and classroom rules, complete homework or projects on time, and put forth effort in their work. They are able to manage their time, their behavior and organize and keep track of their materials. Students who are ready to learn come prepared for class. They are on time, they have the materials they will need, and they have completed homework assignments so they are ready to go in class.

 

All classrooms use a card flip system for behavior management. If a student is off track a reminder is given – the concerning behavior is identified and paired with the desirable behavior that should replace it. A second reminder is given if the student continues. A card flip to yellow occurs on the third reminder. This is a visual caution sign to the student reminding him/her that trouble is approaching. If the student continues the inappropriate behavior the card is flipped to red. A recess is missed and a note is put into the planner describing the behavior. A blue card flip is an office visit and a phone call home. More than one recess may be lost. At the end of the month a PBIS celebration is held. Students who do not have more than 6 yellows and 4 reds may attend the monthly celebration. * Please note: if a homework assignment is not turned in on time a student is required to stay in for recess until the assignment is completed.

 

Class Dojo is used throughout the day to reward students for on task behaviors. Class Dojo is set up so it is a running total of positive and negative points. Students may earn points individually and as a class. Individual points will be redeemed on Fridays. Class points will be redeemed at the end of a month. On Mondays, students will start out at zero points. At the beginning of a month class points are reset. If a student or the class does something positive they receive a point. If a student or the does something negative they lose one point.Parents will receive a weekly report on their student’s behavior if they have signed up for a Class Dojo parent account. Students may bring a healthy snack each day. Examples of healthy snacks are sliced fruits or vegetables, hummus and pita bread/chips, yogurt, applesauce, cheese and crackers, muffins.

Testing

Students in grades 1 – 3 will take the NWEA reading and math tests in the fall, winter and spring . Students in grades 1 – 3 will also be assessed with the Fountas & Pinnell system. This is done in fall and spring to determine a student’s just right reading level.

 

Third grade students take the MEAP for reading and math. A MEAP review is completed several weeks before the test is administered.

 

Students in grades 1 – 3 will also take the Grosse Pointe Writing test. Practice writing assessments are given throughout the year.

 

Ongoing assessments are given in math, science and social studies. Testing may take the form of a quiz after a section of material is covered, or it may take the form of a test after a unit has been completed. Ongoing asessments are often differentiated in format, content, and length in order to fit the needs of students and accurately assess student understanding. These ongoing assessments are a key in driving daily instruction.

 

Testing dates will be posted on the classroom calendar, and reminders will be given in classroom newsletters.
